Part A · At the Airport機場接機

I. 英文原文＋逐句翻譯

Joseph is at the airport to pick up a client from Canada.
J: Joseph　T: Tonya

Joseph 在機場要接一位從加拿大來的客戶。
J: Excuse me. Are you Tonya from Origin Publishers?
J：不好意思，請問你是 Origin 出版社的 Tonya 嗎？
T: Yes. I'm Tonya from Origin Publishers.
T：是的，我是 Origin 出版社的 Tonya。
J: It's nice to finally meet you. I'm Joseph. May I help you with your luggage?
J：很高興終於見到你。我是 Joseph。我可以幫你拿行李嗎？
T: Yes, please. Thanks for giving me a hand, Joseph.
T：好的，麻煩你了。謝謝你幫我一把，Joseph。
J: It's no problem. So, how was your flight? I hope you had a pleasant trip.
J：沒問題。對了，你的飛行旅程如何？希望你有個愉快的旅程。
T: It was OK. The food didn't taste very good, but I think that's normal for food served on an airplane.
T：還可以。食物不太好吃，不過我想飛機上供應的食物大概都這樣。
J: That's true. Did you get to watch any movies?
J：沒錯。你有看任何電影嗎？
T: No. I slept most of the time. Traveling always makes me tired.
T：沒有。我大部分時間都在睡覺。旅行總是讓我覺得很累。
J: With such a long trip, that's not surprising. Well, we could go if you're ready. We have a car outside waiting to take us to your hotel.
J：這麼長的旅程，這也不意外。如果你準備好了，我們就可以出發。外面有車在等我們，會載我們去你的飯店。
T: Great. I'd like to put all my stuff away and relax for a bit.
T：太好了。我想把我的東西放好，稍微休息一下。

II. 重要單字

單字KK音標詞性中文變化型 / 同反義字
publisher[ˈpʌblɪʃɚ]n.出版商複 publishers ／ 同 press
luggage ⭐[ˈlʌɡɪdʒ]n.行李不可數！永遠單數 ／ 同 baggage
flight[flaɪt]n.飛行；班機複 flights ／ 同 trip, journey
pleasant[ˈplɛzn̩t]adj.愉快的；舒適的同 enjoyable, nice ／ 反 unpleasant
taste[test]v.嚐起來；味道tasted / tasted / tasting
normal[ˈnɔrml̩]adj.正常的同 usual, regular ／ 反 abnormal
airplane[ˈɛrˌplen]n.飛機複 airplanes ／ 同 plane, aircraft
surprising[sɚˈpraɪzɪŋ]adj.令人驚訝的同 amazing ／ 反 expected
relax[rɪˈlæks]v.放鬆relaxed / relaxed / relaxing ／ 同 rest
client[ˈklaɪənt]n.客戶複 clients ／ 同 customer
aisle ⭐[aɪl]n.走道s 不發音！冠詞用 an
coach[kotʃ]n.經濟艙= economy class
purchase[ˈpɝtʃəs]v.購買同 buy（老師：like = purchase = buy）
stuff[stʌf]n.東西（口語）不可數 ／ 同 things

III. 重要片語

  • pick up接（某人） 受詞為代名詞時必須放中間：pick him up（O）／pick up him（X） Joseph is at the airport to pick up a client.
  • give sb a hand幫某人一把 = help sb；結構：give + 人 + a hand Thanks for giving me a hand, Joseph.
  • put . . . away把……收起來 受詞為代名詞時須放中間：put it away（O） I'd like to put all my stuff away.
  • for a bit一下子；一會兒 = for a while / for a moment I'd like to relax for a bit.
  • would you mind + V-ing你介意……嗎？ ⭐⭐⭐ mind 後面只能接動名詞 (V-ing)，絕對不可接不定詞！ Would you mind helping me with my luggage?

IV. 文法重點

1. ⭐⭐⭐ mind + V-ing（介意做某事）
❌ Would you mind to help me?（錯）
⭕ Would you mind helping me?（對）
回答：不介意 → No, not at all. / 介意 → Sorry, I can't.
2. 過去式否定 didn't + 原形動詞
The food didn't taste very good.（didn't 後動詞要回原形，不可寫 didn't tasted）
3. 動名詞當主詞 → 動詞用單數
Traveling always makes me tired.（不是 make）
規則：V-ing 當主詞時，動詞用單數。
4. such + a + adj. + N 的用法
With such a long trip, that's not surprising.
5. 不可數名詞：luggage / food / stuff / baggage
這些字沒有複數形，不可加 s，前面也不加 a/an。
6. by + 交通工具（不加冠詞）
by plane / by car / by taxi ⭕　　by a plane ❌
📌 老師強調的紅色警示考點：
① luggage 永遠是單數！（不可數名詞，沒有複數形）
② like = purchase = buy（三個動詞同義）
③ aisle 的「s」不發音，且冠詞用 an（an aisle seat）
④ mind + V-ing（老師在課本上圈起來強調）

Part B · Accommodation飯店入住

I. 英文原文＋逐句翻譯

聽力填空選項：
A. That's fine with me.　B. That sounds good.　C. Here you go.　D. Thank you very much.

Joseph is helping Tonya check in at the hotel.
R: Receptionist　J: Joseph　T: Tonya

Joseph 在幫 Tonya 辦理飯店入住。
R: Welcome to Royal Inn. How can I help you?
R：歡迎光臨皇家旅館。有什麼我可以幫忙的嗎？
J: Hello. This is Ms. Tonya Hoffman, and she would like to check in.
J：你好。這位是 Tonya Hoffman 小姐，她要辦理入住。
R: Good afternoon, Ms. Hoffman. I'll just need to see your passport.
R：午安，Hoffman 小姐。我需要看一下您的護照。
T: Here you go. (hands over passport)
T：拿去吧。（遞出護照）
R: All right. And how will you be paying for the room?
R：好的。請問您要怎麼支付房費？
J: All the charges should go on my company credit card. (hands over credit card)
J：所有費用都記在我的公司信用卡上。（遞出信用卡）
R: OK. You are in room 16-G. Here is your key card. The elevators are to your left.
R：好的。您的房間是 16-G。這是您的房卡。電梯在您的左手邊。
T: Thank you very much.
T：非常感謝你。
J: Now that you are checked in, I'll leave you here for a while. How about I pick you up at 6:00 for dinner?
J：既然你已經辦好入住手續了，我就先讓你休息一下。我六點來接你吃晚餐如何？
T: Sure. That sounds good.
T：好的。聽起來不錯。
J: And tomorrow we'll do the company tour. Will 9:00 a.m. be OK?
J：然後明天我們要參觀公司。早上九點可以嗎？
T: That's fine with me.
T：我沒問題。

II. 重要單字

單字KK音標詞性中文變化型 / 同反義字
inn[ɪn]n.旅館（小型）複 inns ／ 同 hotel
passport[ˈpæsˌpɔrt]n.護照複 passports
charge[tʃɑrdʒ]n.費用；收費複 charges ／ 同 fee, cost
elevator[ˈɛləˌvetɚ]n.電梯複 elevators ／ 同 lift（英式）／ 反 stairs
receptionist[rɪˈsɛpʃənɪst]n.接待員複 receptionists
accommodation[əˌkɑməˈdeʃən]n.住宿同 lodging, housing
while[hwaɪl]n.一會兒同 moment, period
badge ⭐[bædʒ]n.識別證；徽章老師補充字 ／ 同 ID, name tag
business card—n.名片= name card
cash[kæʃ]n.現金不可數 ／ 反 credit card
vacation[veˈkeʃən]n.假期同 holiday ／ 反 work

III. 重要片語

  • check in辦理入住／報到 反義為 check out（退房）；老師補充：也用於圖書館借還書 She would like to check in.
  • hand over交出；遞給 hand + 受詞 + over（可分） Can you please hand over your ID?
  • now that + 子句既然…… 連接詞，後面接完整句子 Now that you are checked in, I'll leave you here.
  • for a while一段時間 for + 時間長度 I'll leave you here for a while.
  • How about + N／子句 ?……如何？ 提議用語 How about I pick you up at 6:00?
  • punch in / punch out上班打卡／下班打卡 ⭐ 老師補充的職場片語 Workers must punch in before 9 a.m.

IV. 文法重點

1. Now that + 子句（既然……）
Now that you are checked in, I'll leave you here for a while.
結構：Now that + 完整子句，表「既然……（那就……）」
2. 未來進行式 will be + V-ing
How will you be paying for the room?
（= How will you pay for the room? 但較禮貌）
3. 條件句 If + 現在式, 主詞 + will . . .
If you don't have your passport, you won't be allowed on the plane.
規則：If 子句用現在式，主句用未來式。
4. 比較級 + than
Using the stairs is healthier than taking the elevator.
5. 四個社交回應用語（必背）
Here you go. → 遞東西時說
Thank you very much. → 收到東西／服務時說
That sounds good. → 同意提議時說
That's fine with me. → 對時間／安排沒意見時說
6. Ms. / Mr. / Mrs. / Miss 稱謂區別
Ms. → 女士（不分婚姻）　　Mrs. → 太太（已婚）
Miss → 小姐（未婚）　　Mr. → 先生
📌 老師補充重點：
① badge（識別證）— 與 passport / ID 相關概念
② punch in / punch out（上班／下班打卡）— 職場必備用語
③ check in 在圖書館也表示「借還書」
④ To Whom It May Concern → 敬啟者（商業書信開頭）
⑤ Sincerely → 敬上（商業書信結尾）

Part C · A Company Tour公司參訪

I. 英文原文＋逐句翻譯

聽力填空選項：
A. in charge of　B. meeting　C. on the agenda　D. pleasant

Joseph takes Tonya on a tour of his company.
J: Joseph　T: Tonya　M: Megan　S: Sam

Joseph 帶 Tonya 參觀他的公司。
J: Welcome, Tonya. I hope you had a pleasant evening.
J：歡迎你，Tonya。希望你昨晚過得 愉快。
T: I did. Thank you very much. What's on the agenda for today?
T：是的，非常感謝你。今天 的行程是什麼？
J: I'm going to show you around the office and introduce you to some important people here.
J：我要帶你參觀辦公室，並介紹一些重要的人給你認識。
T: Great. I'm looking forward to meeting your coworkers.
T：太好了。我很期待 認識你的同事們。
J: This is Megan. She's Mr. Jackson's secretary, so when you call the CEO's office, you'll talk to her first.
J：這位是 Megan。她是 Jackson 先生的秘書，所以當你打電話到執行長辦公室時，你會先和她說話。
T: I think I've talked to you before. It's nice to meet you face-to-face.
T：我想我們之前通過電話。很高興能跟你面對面見面。
M: It's nice to finally meet you, too.
M：我也很高興終於見到你。
J: And this is Sam. He's in charge of accounting. He'll go over the details of your company's account with us this afternoon.
J：這位是 Sam。他 負責會計事務。他下午會跟我們仔細討論你們公司帳戶的細節。
T: Pleased to meet you.
T：很高興認識你。
S: Glad to meet you, too. After we finish our business, I'd like to take you to one of my favorite restaurants for lunch.
S：我也很高興認識你。等我們忙完業務後，我想帶你去我最愛的一家餐廳吃午餐。
T: That sounds like fun. I'd love to try out the local food.
T：聽起來很有趣。我很想嘗嘗看當地的食物。

II. 重要單字

單字KK音標詞性中文變化型 / 同反義字
secretary[ˈsɛkrəˌtɛrɪ]n.秘書複 secretaries ／ 同 assistant
face-to-face[fes tə fes]adv.面對面地連字號連接 ／ 同 in person
accounting[əˈkaʊntɪŋ]n.會計不可數 ／ accountant = 會計師（人）
favorite[ˈfevərɪt]adj.最喜愛的同 preferred ／ 反 least favorite
agenda[əˈdʒɛndə]n.議程複 agendas ／ 同 schedule, plan
coworker[ˈkoˌwɝkɚ]n.同事複 coworkers ／ 同 colleague
CEO—n.執行長= Chief Executive Officer
CFO—n.財務長= Chief Financial Officer
COO—n.營運長= Chief Operating Officer
VP—n.副總裁= Vice President
PM—n.專案經理= Project Manager

III. 重要片語

  • show sb around帶某人參觀 show + 人 + around + 地點 I'm going to show you around the office.
  • look forward to + V-ing期待…… ⭐⭐⭐ to 是介系詞，後面要接動名詞 (V-ing)，不是不定詞！ I'm looking forward to meeting your coworkers.
  • be in charge of負責…… of 後面接名詞或動名詞 He's in charge of accounting.
  • go over仔細檢視；說明 go + over（不可分） He'll go over the details with us.
  • try out試用；嘗試 try + 受詞 + out I'd love to try out the local food.
  • would rather + V₁ + than + V₂寧可……也不願…… ⭐ rather 與 than 之後都接原形動詞 I would rather talk face-to-face than talk on the phone.

IV. 文法重點

1. ⭐⭐⭐ look forward to + V-ing（期待……）
❌ I'm looking forward to meet you.（錯）
⭕ I'm looking forward to meeting you.（對）
📌 關鍵：這裡的 to 是介系詞，不是不定詞，所以後面要接動名詞！
2. let + 受詞 + 原形動詞（使役動詞）
Let me take you to the car.
let 後面接原形動詞（不加 to）；類似動詞：make / have
3. be terrible at + N／V-ing（在……方面很差）
I'm terrible at math.
反義：be good at（擅長）
4. one of + 複數名詞
one of my favorite restaurants（不能用 restaurant 單數）
5. sound like + N / sound + adj.
That sounds like fun.（fun 是名詞）
That sounds good.（good 是形容詞）
6. CEO / CFO / COO / VP / PM 職稱縮寫
Chief Executive Officer = 執行長
Chief Financial Officer = 財務長
Chief Operating Officer = 營運長
Vice President = 副總裁
Project Manager = 專案經理

② be in charge of（負責……）

Part D · Showing Hospitality接待款待

I. 英文原文＋逐句翻譯

聽力填空選項：
A. Don't worry.　B. No, I haven't.　C. Thanks for telling me.　D. That would be great.

Sam takes Tonya to a restaurant, and then Joseph helps her buy souvenirs.
S: Sam　T: Tonya　J: Joseph

Sam 帶 Tonya 去餐廳，然後 Joseph 幫她買紀念品。

I. 餐廳場景

I. 餐廳場景
S: Have you ever had this kind of food before?
S：你以前吃過這種食物嗎？
T: No, I haven't. But I'm really looking forward to it.
T：不，我沒有。不過我真的很期待。
S: I want to make this lunch worth your while, so I'll make sure to get you something delicious. Is there anything you don't like to eat?
S：我想讓你這頓午餐物超所值，所以我會確保點一些好吃的給你。有什麼你不喜歡吃的嗎？
T: I don't like very spicy food.
T：我不喜歡太辣的食物。
S: OK. And do you prefer chicken or beef?
S：好的。那你比較喜歡雞肉還是牛肉？
T: Either is fine with me.
T：都可以。

II. 市場購物場景

II. 市場購物場景
J: Is there anything in particular you wanted to see or do today?
J：你今天有特別想看或想做什麼嗎？
T: I do need to pick up some souvenirs for my family and friends.
T：我真的需要買一些紀念品給家人和朋友。
J: If you're up for it, I could take you to an outdoor market.
J：如果你願意的話，我可以帶你去一個露天市場。
T: That would be great.
T：那真是太好了。
J: All right. Let's go to the market. By the way, you are allowed to bargain for the items there. No one ever pays the first amount the seller says.
J：好的，我們走吧。對了，那裡的東西是可以議價的。沒有人會直接付賣家開的第一個價錢。
T: Thanks for telling me. I'm not very good at bargaining though.
T：謝謝你告訴我。不過我不太擅長議價。
J: Don't worry. I'll help you get the best deal.
J：別擔心。我會幫你拿到最好的價錢。

II. 重要單字

單字KK音標詞性中文變化型 / 同反義字
souvenir[ˌsuvəˈnɪr]n.紀念品複 souvenirs ／ 同 memento
delicious[dɪˈlɪʃəs]adj.美味的同 tasty, yummy ／ 反 disgusting
spicy[ˈspaɪsɪ]adj.辛辣的同 hot ／ 反 mild, bland
outdoor[ˈaʊtˌdɔr]adj.戶外的；露天的同 open-air ／ 反 indoor
bargain[ˈbɑrɡɪn]v. / n.議價；划算同 haggle, negotiate
though[ðo]adv.不過；雖然同 however ／ 放句尾
deal[dil]n.交易；划算的價格複 deals ／ 同 bargain, agreement
hospitality[ˌhɑspɪˈtælətɪ]n.款待；好客不可數 ／ 反 hostility
either[ˈiðɚ]pron./adj./adv.（兩者中）任一反 neither
prefer[prɪˈfɝ]v.比較喜歡同 like better ／ 反 dislike

III. 重要片語

  • worth one's while值得；物超所值 Going to graduate school would be worth your while.
  • in particular特別地 There's nothing in particular I want to eat.
  • be up for + N／V-ing願意做…… Are you up for planning the meeting?
  • by the way順帶一提 轉換話題用；簡寫 BTW By the way, the meeting is moved to Friday.
  • feel like + V-ing想要做…… feel like 後面只接動名詞，不接不定詞！ I feel like eating pizza tonight.

IV. 文法重點

1. ⭐⭐⭐ either 的三種用法
① 代名詞：兩者中任一個
　　Either is fine with me.
② 連接詞：either A or B（A 或 B）
　　We have to choose either this one or that one.
③ 副詞：用於否定句句尾，意為「也」
　　I don't know, either.（注意：肯定句的「也」用 too / also）
2. ⭐⭐⭐ prefer 的三種用法
① prefer A or B（選 A 還是 B？）
　　Do you prefer chicken or beef?
② prefer to + V（比較喜歡做……）
　　I prefer to eat out.
③ prefer A to B（喜歡 A 勝過 B）⭐ 注意：用 to，不是 than！
　　Darren prefers French food to Chinese food.
3. ⭐⭐ something + 形容詞（形容詞後置）
I'll get you something delicious.（不能說 delicious something）
規則：something / anything / nothing / everything 後面的形容詞要放在後面。
4. 現在完成式 Have you ever + p.p. + before?
Have you ever had this food before?
肯定答：Yes, I have. ／ 否定答：No, I haven't.
5. be allowed to + V（被允許）
You are allowed to bargain for the items.
6. though 句尾用法（不過、然而）
I'm not good at bargaining though.（= but / however）

① 紅色警示：必考三大句型

⭐⭐⭐ 必考

mind + V-ing（介意做某事）

  • ❌ Would you mind to help me?（錯）
  • ⭕ Would you mind helping me?（對）
  • 📌 mind 後面只能接動名詞，絕對不能接不定詞！
  • 回答：No, not at all.（不介意 → 答應幫忙）
⭐⭐⭐ 必考

look forward to + V-ing（期待……）

  • ❌ I'm looking forward to see you.（錯）
  • ⭕ I'm looking forward to seeing you.（對）
  • 📌 to 在這裡是介系詞，不是不定詞！
  • 類似句型：be used to + V-ing（習慣於……）
⭐⭐⭐ 老師紅色警示

luggage 永遠是單數！

  • luggage 是不可數名詞，沒有複數形
  • ❌ luggages（X）／ a luggage（X）／ two luggages（X）
  • ⭕ 要表達「兩件行李」用 two pieces of luggage 或 two bags
  • 類似不可數名詞：baggage、food、stuff、information

② 黃色警示：易混淆句型

⭐⭐ 易考

either 的三種用法

  • ① 代名詞：Either is fine with me.（兩者皆可）
  • ② 連接詞：either A or B（A 或 B）
  • ③ 副詞（否定句的「也」）：I don't know, either.
  • 📌 注意：肯定句的「也」用 too / also，否定句才用 either！
⭐⭐ 易考

prefer 的三種用法

  • ① prefer A or B：Do you prefer chicken or beef?
  • ② prefer to + V：I prefer to eat out.
  • ③ prefer A to B：I prefer tea to coffee.
  • 📌 ❌ prefer A than B（這是常見錯誤！）
⭐⭐ 易考

something / anything + 形容詞

  • 形容詞要放在後面！
  • ❌ delicious something（X）
  • ⭕ something delicious（O）
  • 同類詞：anything spicy / nothing special / everyone present

⭐ 商業書信

商業書信格式

  • To Whom It May Concern: → 敬啟者（不知道收件人是誰時）
  • Dear Sir or Madam, → 親愛的先生／女士（同義替代）
  • Sincerely, → 敬上（正式結尾）
  • Yours truly, / Best regards, → 其他正式結尾用語

④ 重要時態總整理

時態

本課出現的 6 種時態

  • 過去式：The food didn't taste good.（didn't + 原形）
  • 現在完成式：Joseph has arrived.（has/have + p.p.）
  • 現在完成進行式：Kate has been talking for a while.
  • 未來式：I'll help you.（will + 原形）
  • 未來進行式：How will you be paying?（will be + V-ing）
  • 被動式：You are allowed to bargain.（be + p.p.）
